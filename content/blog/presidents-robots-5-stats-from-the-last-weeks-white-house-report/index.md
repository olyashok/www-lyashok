[Economic Report of the President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/default/files/docs/ERP_2016_Book_Complete%20JA.pdf) was published last week. For the first time ever it had a whole chapter on robotics. Some stats in there are non-trivial.

- $20hr jobs have 83% chance of being automated. 31% for $20-40 and just 4% for >$40hr.

![Image](/media/linkedin/presidents-robots-5-stats-from-the-last-weeks-white-house-report/inline-01.jpg)

- $12-$18/hr jobs are disappearing fast. $12 jobs are growing - these are mostly jobs requiring physical presence.

![Image](/media/linkedin/presidents-robots-5-stats-from-the-last-weeks-white-house-report/inline-02.jpg)

- 29% of jobs now require occupational license of some sort. This is now a very significant barrier. Licensed workers make 10-15% more.

- $15b is the size of 2014 on-demand economy according PwC. McKinsey Global Institute predicts +$2.7 trillion to global GDP by 2025 form on-demand economy.
- Productivity growth has slowed from it's highest in mid-20th century.

![Image](/media/linkedin/presidents-robots-5-stats-from-the-last-weeks-white-house-report/inline-03.jpg)

Interesting read overall, very bullish on growth.
