167-page [Future of Jobs report](http://www3.weforum.org/docs/Media/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_embargoed.pdf) from World Economic Forum is out.

## Education

- 65% of children entering primary school today will ultimately end up working in completely new job types that don’t yet exist yet
- 50% of subject knowledge acquired during the first year of a four-year technical degree outdated by the time students graduate

## On AI

- Artificial intelligence and machine learning is #7 on the list of tech drivers of change
- AI is viewed as eliminating -1.56% of jobs till 2020

![Image](/media/linkedin/10-data-points-from-the-new-2020-future-of-jobs-world-economic-forum-report/inline-01.jpg)

## On Crowdsourcing

- Crowdsourcing, the sharing economy and peer-to-peer platforms is #5 on disruptors list
- Leveraging flexible working arrangements and online talent platforms is viewed as an imperative tool for HR

## Net effect

- Net employment impact of more than 5.1 million jobs lost to disruptive labour market changes over the period 2015–2020
- Total loss of 7.1 million jobs— two thirds of which are concentrated in the Office and Administrative job family
- Total gain of 2 million jobs, in several smaller job families
